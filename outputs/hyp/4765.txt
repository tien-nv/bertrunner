for more than 2,000 years , invaders have been fighting over glorious kefalonia.
fortunately , the present settlers - the greek themselves and the odd expat - have stoutly resisted the encroachment of mass tourism.
so instead of fast - food restaurants you will find charming family - run tavernas that make this island a foodie heaven.
great forests of pines , cypress and cedars still sweep down the steep hillsides , often ending in cliffs of chalk as white as those at dover , or in fine shingle and sand beaches , fringing a sea whose warm , sun - reflecting waters are of startling clarity

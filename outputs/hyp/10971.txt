the number of new hiv infections in a rural indiana county has grown , according to the cdc.
the institute is working with state health leaders to control the " severe outbreak , " which has spread among users of a prescription opioid called opana.
as of friday , 142 people have tested positive for hiv , with 136 confirmed cases and six more with preliminary positive test results , all in rural scott county and jackson counties.
the cdc and state health leaders held a news conference friday to talk about the new numbers and about the growing threat of the spread of disease from iv drug use , especially in isolated rural areas that have sparse health resources

the parents of an 11 - year - old autistic boy who miraculously survived four - and - a - half - days alone in the bush after he vanished from their favourite campground have vowed to continue holidaying at the camping spot.
luke shambrook , who went without food or water since good friday , has been going to the candlebark campground at lake eildon national park , northeast of melbourne , since he was just one year old , his parents rachel and tim shambrook revealed in an emotional press conference on thursday.
a desperate search for luke shambrook in the park was mounted after he disappeared from the campground on friday.
the moment luke shambrook was found was captured on video , he was seen peering out from behind trees before a uniformed police officer reached him and took his hand to reassure him he was finally safe

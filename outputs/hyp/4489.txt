an obscure agreement struck by state governments in 1993 means the vast majority of australian will not get a day off this anzac day weekend.
under the pact , no public holiday is triggered when anzac day falls on a saturday , as it does this year.
the decision not to grant the holiday has been condemned by some groups and human resources expert dundon warned there would be a big jump in employees calling in sick because they feel ' entitled ' to the day off , reports courier mail.
the anniversary of the landing on gallipoli has fallen on a saturday this year , meaning all states but western australia will miss out on holiday on monday australian state governments came together for a council of australian governments meeting in 1993 to try and make public holidays consistent across the nation
